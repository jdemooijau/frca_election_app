# Church Order Notes

Brief notes on how this app aligns with the FRCA Church Order and the typical election process used in FRCA congregations.

## Church Order Article 3

The FRCA Church Order, Article 3, governs the appointment of office bearers (elders and deacons). The typical process is:

1. **Nomination by the congregation** — the congregation is invited to submit names of brothers they consider suitable for office.
2. **Slate drawn up by the consistory with deacons** — the consistory with the deacons (E+D) considers the nominations and draws up a slate of candidates, typically twice the number of vacancies.
3. **Pulpit announcement** — the slate is announced from the pulpit, and the congregation is given opportunity to lodge any lawful objections.
4. **Election** — the male confessing members (brothers) gather after a service to vote on the slate. Each brother may vote for up to the number of vacancies per office.
5. **Counting and appointment** — the votes are counted. The consistory with the deacons verifies the count and appoints the elected brothers.
6. **Approbation** — the names of the elected brothers are announced to the congregation. If no lawful objections are lodged during the approbation period, the brothers are ordained and installed.

## How the App Fits the Process

This app covers **step 4 (the election)** and **step 5 (counting)**. It does not handle nominations, slate drawing, pulpit announcements, or approbation — those are the responsibility of the consistory with the deacons.

| Step | Handled by |
|------|-----------|
| Nomination | Consistory with deacons (outside the app) |
| Drawing up the slate | Consistory with deacons (outside the app) |
| Pulpit announcement | Minister/chairman (outside the app) |
| **Election (voting)** | **This app** |
| **Counting** | **This app** |
| Appointment | Consistory with deacons (outside the app) |
| Approbation | Consistory with deacons (outside the app) |
| Ordination | Minister (outside the app) |

## Election Rules

Each FRCA congregation may have its own local election rules that specify details such as:

- The number of candidates per vacancy (typically 2:1)
- The threshold for election (typically more than half the valid votes divided by the number of vacancies, and at least two-fifths of participating voters)
- Whether and how second rounds are conducted
- How paper and digital votes are combined

This app supports the common patterns (multiple rounds with candidate carry-forward, combined paper and digital counting) but does not enforce any specific congregation's rules. The consistory with the deacons retains authority over interpreting the results and applying their local election rules.

## What the App Does Not Do

- **Does not replace the attendance register** — the physical attendance register is always used alongside the app
- **Does not determine who is elected** — the app reports vote counts; the consistory with the deacons determines whether the threshold has been met according to their rules
- **Does not handle the approbation process** — that is a separate congregational matter
- **Does not store any information about the denomination's polity** — it is a simple voting tool that happens to use FRCA terminology

## Further Reading
