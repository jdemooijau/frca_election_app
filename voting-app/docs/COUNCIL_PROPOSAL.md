# Office Bearer Election — Proposed Change to Voting Method

*A proposal for consideration by the Church Council*

## The proposal

We propose that the office bearer election continue to take place at the
traditional in-person meeting after the morning service on election Sunday,
but that votes may be cast **digitally on brothers' phones as an alternative
to paper ballots**. Paper ballots will remain available for any brother who
prefers them or does not have a phone.

The benefits are **time saving and accurate counts**, with results
displayed on the liturgy boards immediately after voting closes.

## How it will work

1. Brothers gather after the morning service, as they do now
2. Each brother signs the attendance register at the door and is handed
   either a voting code slip (if voting by phone) or a printed paper ballot
   (if voting on paper)
3. Brothers vote on their own phones or on paper, whichever they prefer
4. Paper ballots are tallied manually and entered into the system
5. Results are calculated immediately and displayed on the liturgy boards
6. If a second round is needed, it is run the same way, immediately, with
   fresh ballots

## What does not change

The nomination process, the candidate announcement from the pulpit, the
voting thresholds (Article 6), the approbation period, and the ordination
all remain exactly as they are. The only change is the method by which votes
are cast on election Sunday.

## Safety net

Paper ballots and the attendance register are always physically present in
the room. If the technology fails for any reason — power outage, WiFi
failure, or any other issue — the election reverts immediately to the
traditional paper ballot process, exactly as we do now.

## Live demonstration at this meeting

A working demonstration of the proposed system will be set up in the
meeting room. Each council member will receive a sample ballot showing
**both** voting options side by side:

- A traditional paper ballot on the top half
- An online code ballot with a QR code on the bottom half

You will be invited to try either (or both) methods during the discussion,
to experience how the system works in practice. The candidates are
fictional Dutch names and the election is clearly labelled as a
demonstration — no real votes will be recorded.

**Please bring a smartphone to the meeting if possible** so you can try the
online voting experience firsthand. Paper ballots will also be available
for those who prefer them or forget their phone.

## Decisions requested from the council

1. Approval in principle of digital voting on election Sunday, with paper
   as an alternative
2. Confirmation of the task team responsible for setting up, testing, and
   running the system
3. Agreement on a suitable date for a full User Acceptance Test (UAT) dry
   run with the church council using dummy candidates, before the real
   election

If the UAT does not pass to the council's satisfaction, this proposal is
not approved and the election will proceed on paper as it does now.
